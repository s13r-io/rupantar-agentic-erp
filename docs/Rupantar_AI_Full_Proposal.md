## 2.3 The Product (AI Agents)

**AI agents run on WhatsApp and voice and handle daily shop operations autonomously — no separate app, no dashboard the owner must learn.**

**The shopkeeper speaks or sends a photo; the agent executes billing, inventory, credit, and confirmations without a traditional user interface.**

**One interaction channel (WhatsApp) matches how tens of millions of Indian SMBs already coordinate with customers and suppliers.**

Rupantar's product is not a screen-based ERP. It is a bundle of specialised agents that interpret vernacular input, act on shop data, and reply on the same thread the shopkeeper already uses. Positioning aligns with documented behaviour: **WhatsApp is already the default business inbox for a large share of Indian micro and small enterprises** (IAMAI–Kantar India Internet reports on messaging-led commerce and digital adoption among non-metro users).

### 2.3.1 How It Works

The shopkeeper sends a **voice note in Hindi or Hinglish** (or a photo, or a one-word reply). An **AI agent** parses intent, reads catalog and customer context, and **generates the bill, updates inventory, records credit (udhaar), or triggers procurement** as required. The agent then **sends a WhatsApp confirmation** to the owner and, where appropriate, to the customer. The full loop is **voice notes, photos, and minimal typed replies** — no forms, no menus, no training on "where to click." Execution is anchored in **WhatsApp Business Platform capabilities** (text, media, templates, interactive messages) as documented by Meta for business messaging.

### 2.3.2 Vertical-Specific Agent Bundles

The same product pattern — **autonomous agents on WhatsApp/voice** — repeats across verticals; only the bundle composition and vocabulary change.

| Vertical | Agent Bundle | Example Interaction |
|---|---|---|
| Kirana Store | Billing + Inventory + Credit + Procurement + Daily Report | Owner says *"Ram ne 500 ka saman liya udhar mein"* → agent records **credit**, updates **inventory**, sends **Ram a WhatsApp confirmation** |
| Auto Garage | Job Card + Parts Inventory + Customer Update + Vehicle History | Customer WhatsApps **photo of damaged vehicle** → agent opens **job card**, links **vehicle/history**, tracks repair status |
| Pharmacy | Prescription Reader + Drug Interaction + Expiry Watch + Refill Reminder | Customer sends **prescription photo** → agent extracts items, runs **interaction checks**, supports dispense workflow, schedules **refill** |
| Restaurant | Multi-Channel Orders + Kitchen Intelligence + Aggregator Reconciler + Morning Brief | Orders from **counter, Swiggy, Zomato** flow in → agent **routes by prep time**, surfaces kitchen load, **reconciles payouts** daily |
| Salon | Booking + Client Memory + Empty-Slot Filler + Staff Commission | Client messages for **appointment** → agent checks **stylist availability**, confirms, recalls **last visit preferences** (e.g. colour formula), updates commission logic |

### 2.3.3 Zero-UI Design Philosophy

**There is no app install requirement for the shopkeeper and no screen hierarchy to memorise.** The agent meets the user on **WhatsApp**, which already sits on the owner's phone. **Training burden approaches zero** for the shopkeeper because the interaction model mirrors informal chat. Each agent runs **autonomously** within policy; the owner's typical input ceiling is a **voice note or a photo**. This design choice tracks **observed SMB resistance to dedicated app adoption** in India (repeatedly noted in MSME digitalisation surveys and industry SMB software uptake studies — KenResearch / MSME Ministry market commentary on informal retail tooling).

### 2.3.4 Multi-Agent Orchestration

A **single message often spans multiple domains** (e.g. *"bill bana do, stock check karo, aur Ram ka khata update karo"*). An **orchestrator** classifies intent, **routes sub-tasks to specialist agents** (billing, inventory, ledger), and **composes one coherent WhatsApp reply**. Ambiguous or multi-step requests stay in one thread; the orchestrator handles **sequencing and failure recovery** without forcing the shopkeeper into a menu tree. The pattern matches **multi-agent orchestration** described in enterprise AI practice (Gartner guidance on composite AI and agentic workflows) adapted for **low-latency, mobile-first** threads.

### 2.3.5 The Vernacular NLP Challenge

Indian shop-floor language is **not textbook Hindi**. It bundles **local product aliases** (*parle g*, *khokha*, *peti*, *tola*), **Hinglish code-switching**, **regional weights and measures**, and **dialect-specific trade vocabulary**. **Global general-purpose LLMs underperform on this commercial dialect layer** relative to English-centric benchmarks; closing the gap requires **training and evaluation on real shop interactions** (consistent with findings in Indic and code-mixed NLP research, AI4Bharat work on mixed-language understanding). **This gap is the hardest technical problem in the product and the deepest durable advantage** once proprietary interaction and transaction data compound.

## 2.4 The Platform (Agent Builder)

**Without the Agent Builder, every shop deployment implies engineer time — unit economics collapse before scale.**

**With Agent Builder, a trained non-developer partner can stand up a working agent configuration in under 60 minutes.**

**Four layers — library, visual flows, mobile config, and optional code — separate "repeatable deployment" from "custom engineering."**

The platform is how Rupantar turns the product into a **repeatable, partner-led rollout**. Analogues in horizontal automation (n8n, Dify, Langflow) prove demand for visual orchestration; none are optimised for **Indian retail semantics, GST-aware flows, WhatsApp-native execution, and field partners with two days of training**.

### 2.4.1 Four-Layer Architecture

| Layer | What It Is | Primary User | Analogous To |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1: Agent Library** | **160+** pre-built, tested agent nodes organised by vertical and function; each exposes standard inputs/outputs | Partners deploying **out-of-the-box bundles**; shop live in **under 15 minutes** for standard packages | Salesforce AppExchange / Zapier template libraries |
| **L2: Visual Flow Builder** | Drag-and-drop canvas: connect nodes, set triggers (WhatsApp message, UPI credit, timer, stock threshold), shop parameters; **no code** | Partners handling **~80%** of implementations after **2-day** training | n8n canvas / Dify workflow builder |
| **L3: Config Studio** | Mobile-first wizard: catalog, suppliers, customers, language, tone — converts raw shop facts into **agent context** | Partners on-site during the **60-minute** visit; **4G**-tolerant | Shopify store setup wizard |
| **L4: Pro Code SDK** | **Python / JavaScript** SDK: custom nodes, non-standard APIs, publish templates to marketplace; higher margin tier | Tier **3–4** technical partners building vertical specialisations | n8n community nodes / Dify plugins |

### 2.4.2 Core Capabilities

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| WhatsApp-Native Execution | Actions routed through **WhatsApp Business API**: text, **voice notes**, images, buttons, **payment links** |
| Vernacular NLP Engine | Tuned for **Indian commercial vocabulary** across **12 languages**; resolves units and aliases (*adha kilo*, *khokha*, *peti*, *tola*) |
| Offline-First Queue | Work **queues offline**; sync with conflict handling on reconnect — designed for **Tier 2–4** connectivity variance (TRAI telecom performance reports) |
| Planner → Executor → Verifier | Each agent **plans**, **acts**, **verifies**; retries with escalation on failure |
| Confidence Threshold | Below-threshold actions trigger **owner confirmation**; **high-stakes** operations default to **approval** |
| Observability | Audit trail via **Langfuse / OpenTelemetry**; full trace within **48 hours** for disputes |
| Persistent Shop Memory | Isolated memory per shop: customers, catalog, suppliers, history, seasonality — **compounds monthly** |
| Multi-Agent Orchestration | Orchestrator classifies intent, routes to specialists, merges cross-domain replies into one WhatsApp thread |

### 2.4.3 Revenue Model

**Platform subscription** for **Tier 2+** builder partners is **₹999/month** (price point set for partner affordability vs. services margin; comparable to low-tier SaaS seat pricing in India SMB software benchmarks). **Marketplace royalties** on community templates split **30% Rupantar / 70% author** — in line with app store and template marketplace take-rate conventions.

### 2.4.4 Competitive Differentiation

**n8n**, **Dify**, and **Langflow** are strong horizontal automation and LLM orchestration platforms. None ship Indian retail **vernacular** understanding (*khokha*, *peti*, GST-adjacent flows), first-class **WhatsApp Business API** execution for shop operations, or a **partner training path measured in days** for pharma MRs and Tally resellers. Rupantar is **purpose-built for India's retail context** and for **non-developer field partners**; horizontal tools remain analogues, not substitutes.

**Five moats, each independently valuable, together forming a compounding defensive position that deepens every month.**

### Tier 1 — Strategic Insight Paragraphs (Six Verticals)

## 4.3 Platform Architecture (Technical Reference)

**This section is reference material for technically-minded participants.**
**The product definition — what the Agent Builder is and why it matters — is in §2.4.**
**What follows is engineering implementation detail: decisions, capabilities, and integration boundaries.**

### Architecture Decisions

Stack is **cloud-native**, with **LLM providers abstracted** behind an internal interface so models can be swapped or multi-sourced without product-level rewrites. **WhatsApp Business API** is the primary execution channel for shop- and customer-facing actions. **Data residency:** all Indian shop and transaction data on **Indian servers** — consistent with DPDP compliance posture.

### Core Capabilities (Technical Reference)

| Capability | Product Summary (§2.4) | Implementation Notes |
|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp-native execution | Text, voice, images, buttons, payment links — no consumer app | Webhook ingestion, message templating, media pipeline, rate-limit handling per Meta policy |
| Vernacular NLP | **12** languages; Indian commercial vocabulary | Routing to tuned prompts/adapters per locale; evaluation sets from live shop corpora |
| Offline-first queue | Queue locally; sync on reconnect | **CRDTs** (conflict-free replicated data types) for high-write counters; merge rules for cart + ledger events |
| Planner → Executor → Verifier | Plan, act, confirm outcome | Stateful run records; retry and escalation policies per agent class |
| Confidence threshold | Human confirm below threshold; high-stakes always approved | Policy table per action type; audit log of overrides |
| Observability | Full trace for disputes and partner QA | **Langfuse** for LLM traces; **OpenTelemetry** for service graphs; **48h** replay window |
| Persistent shop memory | Isolated memory per tenant | Partitioned store; encryption at rest; retention policy aligned to DPDP |
| Multi-agent orchestration | Intent routing and composition | Orchestrator service; specialist agents as independently deployable units |

### API Boundaries

External systems integrate through **documented REST and webhook** contracts. Data export in **standard formats** (CSV, JSON, accounting interchange) so shops and CAs are not locked in opaquely. The boundary set is designed so **Horizon 2 ecosystem** integrations (banks, brands, ONDC, finance) attach **without re-architecture** — new adapters, not new cores.

## 5.2 What We Will NOT Build

**Scope discipline matches product ambition: explicit exclusions reduce stakeholder pull into adjacent, capital-heavy businesses.**

**Rupantar stays on the operational digital layer; payments, credit books, fleets, and consumer apps sit with partners or incumbents.**

| What We Will NOT Build | Why | What We Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumer-facing app** | Another app raises friction for end-customers and permanent engineering load | Partner with **WhatsApp Business Platform** — customers stay on channels they already use |
| **Payment gateway** | UPI acceptance is solved by Paytm/PhonePe/BharatPe. Building acceptance is regulated and capital-intensive. | Integrate with existing UPI; add **reconciliation and intelligence** on top |
| **Logistics fleet** | Owning riders and vehicles is a negative-margin, ops-heavy business | Optimise delivery routing and communication; **partner** with hyperlocal delivery networks |
| **Lending book** | Direct lending needs NBFC/banking licence, regulatory capital, credit risk expertise | Enable lending via **NBFC partners** using transaction history as creditworthiness signal; referral commission only |
| **Product marketplace** | Shops sell on WhatsApp/ONDC — a Rupantar marketplace competes with customers | Power the **shop's own** sales channels, not a rival platform |

**We build the operational layer. We partner for everything else.**

### Scope Prevention Principles

1. **"One Customer Rule"** — For Phase 0–2, Rupantar serves the **shopkeeper only**. No brand dashboards, bank integrations, or government reporting tools until the shopkeeper experience is proven at **>85%** 90-day retention.

2. **"Partnership Framework"** — When FMCG brands, banks, or government bodies request integration, a structured framework applies: minimum active shops in geography, latency requirements, pilot validation with **500+** shops, and defined partnership windows (**Phase 3+** only). This blocks ad-hoc scope expansion.
